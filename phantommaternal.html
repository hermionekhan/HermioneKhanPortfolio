Phantom Maternal (2024)

An interactive, hybrid novel which utilises the power of fictive text and 3D spaces in sequence to bring a story to life. This project scored a first (80%)

Created for my Disseration Project and built within Unity Game Engine, Phantom Maternal uses 5 chapters: 1, 3 & 5 in written text and 2 & 4 as interactive 3D representations, to diversify the user's experiene and supplement the story with a multimedia approach.

The story flows linearly, the 3D chapters giving the user spacial context and atmosphere while still enabling them to use their imagination as the story content is rooted in its visuals. The use of both traditional and modern storytelling aims to heighten the user's immersion and introduce demographics to a form they possibly never entertained. This project ambitiously aims to bridge the gap between video game and novel in a literal approach, realised in a single digital format.

Skills Used

  • Game Development, Interaction Design, Lighting and Animation using Unity Game Engine
  • 3D Modelling and Rigging using Blender
  • Narrative Design and Level Design
  • Material Design and Shader Editing using Blender, Adobe Photoshop
  • Project Managment and Iteration using GitHub and the Google Suite
  • User Interface Design using Adobe Photoshop and Figma
  • User Experience testing using Semi-formal Interviews and Play Tests.
Project Report

Story Blurb

George is a responsible young lad at the mere age of ten, but he didn't anticipate having to travel cross-country solo for the first time today. It was an accident of course! He could barely make out which carriage his mother was in before the train started to roll away from the platform. He knew that travelling for the first time by himself is bound to be daunting, but is it supposed to be this creepy? The small station, the crackling speakers, the frail passengers, not to mention his new stoic, red-haired caretaker Francie. And what about this train? It's glorious shining metal facade now seems like an illusion from the interior of cracked wallpaper and institutionalsied decor. But what's this? Something changed, something… obvious. Was the train always going this fast? Were the chairs always made of dark garish wood? And the walls… the walls are different. And over there was something in the field just now… it felt dark and intruding, garish and anticipanting. George didn't like this one bit. And now where on earth is Francie?
