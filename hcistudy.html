How Do Different Types Of Online Media Forms Influence Momentary Emotion In Response To Influencer Content? This study scored a first (88%)

This experiment studied how different types of online media forms influence momentary emotion in response to influencer content. In a 1 X 3 between-participants design, a convenience sample of 36 university students (27 female, 8 male, 1 non-binary) was equally randomised into three media form conditions: long-form, short-form and audio-form. They were asked to record their momentary emotion regarding change (using an Affect Grid) and intensity (using a Positive And Negative Affect Schedule). Both quantitative results were not confident in proving that there is an influence from the type of online media form to momentary emotion (overall alpha level was 0.05), however, the qualitative analysis provides insight into how we can use this study moving forward in the research area of influencer content on user emotions.
