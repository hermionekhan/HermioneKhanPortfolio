Siren Time (2022)

A short horror game where the user must evade the monster in the time given, or perish at its hand. Scored a high second (67%)

Made for my first year module 'Media Production For Interactive Environments', Siren Time was a leap into my first experience of Game Development and Design. Despite its amateruish charm, the short game has a strong grasp of design fundamentals: environment, narratology, atmosphere. systems and interactions. Its strengths lie in the audio engineering, animation and narrative design which all tailor towards the research and context put into the piece.

The learning curve for this project was extremely steep, and while it introduced me to the world of Game Development, it also enabled me to pursue a creative identity that I had never explored before. I am now a fan of the horror-genre as a result and continue to make beta games that push tension, atmosphere and story above all else.

Skills Used

  • Game Development, Envrionment Design, Interaction Design, Level Design, Lighting and Animation using Unity Game Engine
  • 3D Modelling using Blender
  • Project Managment and Iteration using GitHub
Project Report

Context & Research

This project includes the Creepypasta (internet urban legend) Siren Head; a tall, skeletal figure with an echoing siren. This online urban legend reached popularity in 2020, and it's horror-elements seemed to fit a video game well: the echoing siren could be used a proximity feature, building atmosphere and tension. A clocktower is also featured to include a physical representation for the time limit the player needs to survive in. The tower itself is insired by an old clocktower on the Seacroft Estate near Leeds, UK. When I first saw this tower it was dark, cold and a silent night. I was respectfully chilled and thought it would be good to insert real-life inspiration into the game.
