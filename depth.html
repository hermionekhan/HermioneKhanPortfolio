Depth (2023)

An immersive virtual space, built on contextual mystery and curated audio and lighting. Acts as a sample of a larger video game context. This project scored a first (73%).

Created for an Advanced 3D Production module and built using Unity Game Engine, Depth aims to situate a rich virtual environment and provide atmosphere for a wider narrative. With all models created in Blender, and custom materials designed in Adobe Photoshop, the environment within Depth reflects a recently abandoned deep-sea lab. The user is granted contextual clues within the environment's design such as promotional banners for the lab's company 'DEPTH' and the state of emergency the lab has been left in: flashing alarms, warning hollograms and not to mention the looming sea-creature seen in the distance.

Skills Used

  • Environment Design, Level Design, Lighting and Animation using Unity Game Engine
  • 3D Modelling and Rigging using Blender
  • Worldbuilding and Narrative Design
  • Material Design and Shader Editing using Blender, Adobe Photoshop

Project Report

World Building & Context

The dep-sea exploration company 'DEPTH' owns multiple off-grid labs situated in the South Pacific Ocean. The exact location of these labs are unknown, and the only way to access them is by undisclosed access points in shallow areas of the coast. DEPTH deny all allegations of these labs' existence, but their multiple personnel damage claim enquiries have alerted the world's authorities that something is amiss in this tightly guarded corporation. The environment created depicts the scenario of an agent who has recently infiltrated one of DEPTH's Pacific labs, only to discover complete abandonement upon arrival. From the scene, the lab is only recently abadoned, with the alarms and evacuation call still reverberrating on the thick glass walls. As the agent, now player, explores the space, they realise that the lab is researching an elusive subject, depicted through frequeny readings, CCTV footage and a range of measurement tools to create a sense of sophistication and confusion. The various company banners depict slogans and propaganda to give the player a sense of the company's control and influence over their employees. Because the experience has no defining end, the player is given a sense of entrapment and isolation which could possibly be explored in future scenarios.
