Jordan Is Pitiful

Context

I started writing this piece in 2023 when I was on holiday in Thailand and I didn't know it at the time but what I was writing was very critical, hypocritical and unresolved. Despite not having the worries which the main character Jordan presents at the time, I was almost projecting and anticipating what worries I would have in the next stage of my life, which coincidentally is now. I don't think I knew that what I was writing then, would resonate so much with me now, and that is the power that I wanted to convey when continuing this story in the present 2024.

Analysis

Jordan is sour and his narrative voice is very sarcastic, so I wanted to use his inner dialogue to show his change in mindset and ultimate realisation of his situation: yes, he's unemployed and isn't valued in his career world, but for him to reflect on this and remove his personal opinions from the trajectory of his unemployment is what I wanted Jordan to realise. Through speaking with his family, who he seemingly looks down upon and regards as clueless when it comes to digital expertise, I wanted Jordan to humble himself, in that he has no right to belittle his family just because they don't understand his situation as he does. It's a classic take on arrogance through rejection, but within the early career context. Despite unknowingly projecting my irrational anxiety into the character at first, the values and commentary Jordan takes is more like an evil reflection of graduating media students. Synopsis

Jordan is unemployed. He has been for over a year since graduating. Is this his fault? No! Of course not! And them, his family, they just don't understand what he's going through. They couldn't understand how difficult it is navigating an early career in the digital industries. And what's more, they keep pushing his limits, asking stupid questions which only push him further into the oblivion of annoyance. What? What did you say? Oh, where's Jordan right now? He's over there by the pool with cousin Roy. What's that? Is he okay? Well... no, I think he's having to tell his family again how hard it is to be him. Oh, you think he's going to realise? Of course not, he's a hypocrite after all.
